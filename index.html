Beyond a decade of experience across a broad range of open-source technologies for full-stack web development--with a focus on complex component based Single Page Apps--I specialize in building scalable applications for the cloud, web, mobile, and desktop. My front-end arsenal includes Vue.js and React.js, while I'm well versed in state-management with flux based architectures like Redux and Vuex.

My back-end APIs are pragmatic, and the SQL behind them is implicitly fast. So, I can serve a multitude of media streaming over a Web Socket or RESTfully with HTTP really quick. Securing microservices with OAuth2 and JWT is not a problem since asymmetric cryptography is my friend. Database administration with MongoDB or Postgres is another of my strong suits. JavaScript is and has been my focus while at companies like Rakuten USA, Mimecast, Digitas North America, and my current contract with Loomis & Sayles. I'm mostly what you would consider a command-line kind of guy, since vim and zsh are my go-to tools, which I enjoy enriching with .yadr and tmux.

I'm very accustomed to both BDD and TDD with libraries like Jest and Mocha, and their correspondents like Selenium for browser driving or Karma for test running. Blackbox testing and mocking APIs is a regular practice for me while I am constantly branching and rebasing my git repositories. I follow a strict semantic versioning scheme to keep track of my continuous integration, end-to-end tests, and overall test coverage. Most of the time my development workflow is piped directly into a Slack channel or integrated into an internal company tool.

Formally trained in Agile methodologies, I can stand in as a Scrum Master and play poker. I'm also formally trained in Software Engineering, providing background on the software development life-cycle, and am great with mock ups and wireframes that can map directly to actionable user stories written with strict grammar. Using React Native, like with Amica Mutual's Claim App and Tolemi's Iris App--both available in the Apple Store and with Google Play--I'm as much a team player as I am a team leader since I've played both roles in helping deliver these products to market.
